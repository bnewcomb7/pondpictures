Authorized by Rhode Island Boy Scouts

Let’s finally tell the whole story.

The Yawgoog Story is a documentary series and companion book chronicling more than a century of Camp Yawgoog — from its founding in 1916 to today.

In 1965, J. Harold Williams wrote Part 1 of The Yawgoog Story. Others have tried to continue it since — but telling this camp’s full history is a job too big for any one person. It’s going to take all of us.

Our Approach

Why a documentary?

In April 2026, Rhode Island Boy Scouts — the owners of the property — approved an independently produced documentary series and book, financed through the Council. Here’s why film is the right way to preserve this history.

Accessible

Far more people will watch a film than read a book — with a companion book ready for those who want to go deeper.

Funded

Paying skilled people to do this work properly is what makes it realistic — and gives it the best chance of actually being finished.

Preservation

A reason to record testimonies now, and to gather and safeguard historical records — photos, film, and documents — before they’re lost.
