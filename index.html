In 1965, J. Harold Williams wrote Part 1 of The Yawgoog Story, the history of Camp Yawgoog from its founding in 1916 to the present. Several additions have since been attempted, but in reality it will take all of us to tell the complete history of this camp.

In April of 2026, Rhode Island Boy Scouts (the owners of the property) approved a documentary series and book to be produced independently, financed through them. Why a documentary?

Accessibility — More people will watch a documentary, with a companion book if they want more.

Funded — Paying for the work to be done makes it more possible to happen.

Preservation — Something to record testimonies for, amass more historical records (including visual).

Pond Pictures LLC was established by Caleb McPhee for the purpose of this production, and needs your help to make “The Yawgoog Story” possible, just as it took hundreds of passionate, hard-working individuals to not just make Camp Yawgoog what it is, but to keep and improve what it is for over 100 years. In particular we’re looking for:

People to Create This With

Filmmakers, writers, artists, musicians, but also fundraisers, grant writers and business folks.

People With the Story

Those with pictures, videos, written testimony, and connections to those who may have that material.
