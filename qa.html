Q & A

We want this to have a relatively quick turnaround so that the older folks who help tell this story are around to see it. This will ideally take about two years, but we want to be realistic about whether or not the resources are there to make this happen, so the rest of 2026 will be spent assembling people and resources to see if it moves forwards. If it doesn’t, whatever resources we do get will be spent on just producing crucial interviews and preserving important documents and photos so that in the future this has a better chance of getting made.
Narragansett Council and Camp Yawgoog should spend their money and resources continuing their program. The intention is to first fund this project with grants from other historical foundations and the like directed to RIBS, who would then use those funds to pay Pond Pictures LLC to produce. Fundraisers and individual donations would be welcome as well.
This is something we want to make available to view at the Yawgoog Heritage Museum and to eventually purchase on Blu-ray at our camp’s trading post. Free on YouTube is certainly an option down the road but wouldn’t be the first place you find it — we’re seeking more official channels to reach a broader audience (not a BIG streaming service like Netflix but something more local).
